About Us

FACULTY OF COMMUNICATION AND INFORMATION SCIENCE

CIS

The Faculty of Communication and Information Sciences (CIS) was formally established through the decision of Senate at its 193rd (Special) meeting held on June, 2008. Part of that senate decision was the movement to CIS of the Dept of Computer Science from the Faculty of Science; and the movement to CIS of the Dept of Mass Communication from the Faculty of Business and Social Sciences. In addition to the decision to move these two Departments to the new Faculty, three new Departments were created namely (Departments of Library and Information Science, Information and Communication Science, Telecommunication Science).

The Faculty of Communication and Information Sciences is the University’s flagship Faculty designed to take full advantage of multidisciplinary interactions of the science and technologies of computing, information and communication. Its undergraduate curriculum is designed to reflect this and underlies the Faculty’s postgraduate programmes.

OUR DEPARTMENTS

Below is the Objectives and Goals of the Five (5) departments available in the Faculty

COMPUTER SCIENCE

The Department of Computer Science, University of Ilorin is committed to the University’s mission of teaching, learning, research and community development services in the field of Computer Science. It is aimed at producing men and women who are well-trained in all facets of computer Science with the main purpose of meeting national and international needs in the area of technological advancement.

OBJECTIVES:

  • Acquire strong theoretical and practical skills in Computer Science in order to work in any IT-driven environment.

  • Understand and apply the underlying principles of Computer Science to a variety of problem domains.

  • Work in groups and appreciate the dynamic and collaborative nature of problem solving.

  • Blend their Computer Science abilities with skills specific to another domain for solving problems in that domain.

  • Embark on lifelong learning for personal and professional growth.

TELECOMMUNICATION SCIENCE

The department of Telecommunication Science of the University of Ilorin was established in recognition of the fact that telecommunication is one of the main drivers of economic growth and globalization today. As such, the philosophy of the programme is to produce telecommunication specialists who are well grounded in the theory and practice of telecommunications. The world has been experiencing tremendous advances in the field of telecommunications which became evident in Nigeria some years ago. Establishing a Department of Telecommunication Science in the University of Ilorin then, is a response to the need for well-rounded graduates, capable of combining theory, practice and managerial skills at meeting the challenges of the highly specialized and competitive telecommunications industry. The department is fully accredited. The Department’s priority has been ensuring the nation’s ICT development and prosperity by addressing her ICT related challenges through science and technology solutions. Presently the department is having two research laboratories (networking laboratory and wireless communication laboratory). The staff of the department researches are cutting edge of technology, relevant and impactful to modern civilization which testify to the grant awards and patent right by the same.

AIM:

The aim of establishing the Department of Telecommunication is to train competent telecommunication specialists who will meet the challenges of the 21st century in the growing telecommunication industry. It is our objective to train students in the core area of telecommunication that includes:-

  • Network computing and security.

  • Intercontinental digital networks.

  • Wireless communication.

  • Telecommunication software applications.

  • Information and communication technology.

  • Telecommunication planning, analysis and design.

  • Telecommunication planning, analysis and design.

  • Telecommunication management.

MASS COMMUNICATION

Mass Communication Department offers a comprehensive and quality undergraduate professionally oriented programme of training, within the broad philosophy of the University oriented towards character and learning, for all categories of people that wish to pursue a career in public communication through the major forms of Mass Communication such as Journalism, Broadcasting, Advertising and Public Relations for National and Global Development.

OBJECTIVES:

  • Acquire and imbibe the basic principles and skills of news gathering, reporting, writing, editing and preparation for both the print, broadcast and new media.

  • Acquire skills for producing, presenting and directing programmes for the electronic media including radio, television and films.

  • Acquire and imbibe the basic principles and skills for the practise of advertising and public relatios in areas of general campaign management and strategies for all forms of marketing/ communication objectives, consumer behaviours and public relations.

  • Understand the concepts and functions of Mass Communications and the relationship of Mass Communication and Society.

  • Become competent creative and artistic writers and users of language for effective public communication through the mass media.

  • Understand and assimilate the ethics and regulations guiding the practise of Mass Communication in Nigeria in particular and in any other society of the world.

  • Acquire skills for designing and implementing communication/messages for social change.

  • Acquire the basic skills for invesigating the major elements of Mass Communiation.

LIBRARY AND INFORMATION SCIENCE

The Dept of Library and Information Science of the University of Ilorin is committed to the University’s mission of teaching, learning, research and community services. As a professional dept, it educates its students, in both theory and practice, to identify, acquire, organize, and disseminate information. By integrating information technology across the curriculum, the Dept will impart the knowledge and skills of the profession in preserving and providing access to the records of our cultural heritage and the documents of the scholarly researc

OBJECTIVES:

  • Acquire intellectual capabilities to understand and appreciate their environments.

  • Demonstrate creative and innovative abilities in selecting, acquiring, organizing, retrieving and disseminating information.

  • Work in groups and appreciate the dynamic and collaborative nature of problem solving.

  • Disseminate information to users using a variety of information technologies and media formats.

  • Acquire both theoretical and practical skills to work in any information environment.

  • Manage financial, human, information and knowledge resources adequately in any organization.
